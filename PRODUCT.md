# Product

## Register

product

## Users

**Operators** (agency/studio team): manage client projects from the admin side — add clients, create projects, upload deliverables, set phases, manage invoices, write internal notes. Their job is to run a tight, professional engagement without letting anything fall through the cracks.

**Clients**: receive a branded portal window into their engagement. They review deliverables, approve work, track project progress and KPIs, upload reference documents, book meetings, pay invoices, and communicate with the agency. Their job is to stay informed and take action on the things that need their input — without needing to chase anyone down.

The portal is a client-facing window onto the engagement. Clients should feel they are working with a top-tier, highly organized firm.

## Product Purpose

xPortal is a white-label client portal companion to xPM (a project management app). It gives agencies a branded, structured space to engage clients: project hub with KPIs and docs, deliverable approval flows, milestone timelines, invoicing, meeting booking, and file exchange. Success looks like clients who are always informed and never confused, and operators who never have to send a manual "where are we?" update.

## Brand Personality

Polished · Modern · Sharp

Voice: direct, confident, never fluffy. The interface speaks to competent people. Copy is concise and purposeful — no marketing-speak, no over-explanation. Status is always visible. Actions are always clear.

Emotional goal: clients should feel they are in capable hands. They open the portal and immediately understand exactly where their project stands.

## Anti-references

- **Generic SaaS dashboard** (Asana/Jira clones): flat, overcrowded, no visual identity. xPortal has a point of view.
- **Cookie-cutter agency site** (pastel + big serif hero, warm-cream bg): decorative, soft, stock-photo aesthetic. xPortal is a tool, not a brochure.
- **Startup landing page turned tool** (gradient-heavy, hero metrics, glassmorphism): style prioritized over function. Decoration must earn its place.
- **Corporate intranet** (SharePoint aesthetic): dense, grey, bureaucratic. xPortal is premium, not procedural.

## Design Principles

1. **Status at a glance.** The most important thing on any screen is always visible without scrolling: project phase, pending actions, what needs the client's attention.
2. **Actions are never ambiguous.** Every interactive element states exactly what will happen. No vague "Submit" buttons; no mystery icons. Sharp copy, sharp affordances.
3. **The brand is a signal.** Per-client branding (accent color, logo) is the first thing clients see. It signals that this firm cares enough to build a tailored experience — not a generic link dump.
4. **Operators and clients see the same data, not the same UI.** The admin view is dense and configurable; the client view is edited down to what's relevant. Same source of truth, different lenses.
5. **Nothing earns space it doesn't deserve.** No decorative noise, no padding-first layouts. Every visual element either communicates something or gets cut.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA minimum. Dark theme by default (current `bg-primary: #0b0f19`). All interactive elements must have visible focus rings. Body text must meet 4.5:1 contrast; large/bold text 3:1. Reduced motion media query on all transitions and animations.
